## Research Interests

Our research lies at the intersection of **causal inference**, **causal discovery**, and **causal decision-making**, with the goal of building machine learning systems that are not only accurate but also understandable, fair, and actionable.

We develop methods that uncover and utilize causal structures in complex, non-stationary, and high-dimensional environments.
Our core directions include **causal effect identifiability and transportability** — generalizing causal estimates across heterogeneous settings; **causal decision-making** — optimizing actions under uncertainty with causality as a first principle; **causal machine learning** — bridging causal representation learning with modern deep learning; and **causal discovery** — recovering causal graphs from observational, relational, and temporal data.

Beyond these, we are also interested in explainable and trustworthy AI — including fairness, robustness, and interpretability — and in emerging connections between causal reasoning and large-scale foundation models.
